HyperXite EDS / Halbach

Active Maglev Experiment • Halbach Arrays • Mechanical Design

This project focused on the physical design and implementation of a passive electrodynamic suspension (EDS) system for magnetic levitation, utilizing Halbach arrays. I led the design and fabrication of the levitation test rig and magnetic pucks used to experimentally analyze lift, drag, and stability. All components were CAD-modeled in SolidWorks and manufactured via 3D printing and machining. The project was part of UCI’s Hyperloop team, HyperXite. Our subteam, Levitation R&D, won 1st place in Research Excellence at the 2025 Hyperloop Global Conference in Queen’s University, Canada.

CAD & Mechanical Integration

All manufactured parts including circular Halbach array puck, motor interface mount, and wall inserts were designed using SolidWorks. The Test Rig was designed to be a scalable aluminum extrusion frame with linear slide rails, motor mount, and proper mass distribution for optimal data collection.

Fabrication & Assembly

Fully assembled rig includes an aluminum extrusion frame with aluminum plate for levitation. Components were printed using PLA+ and PETG, with machined aluminum for load-bearing links. The aluminum plate minimized eddy current losses and aided heat dissipation.

Results

A Time-of-Flight sensor interfaced with an Arduino and laptop was used to log air gap measurements during levitation. Duty cycle and RPM were controlled using Flipsky ESCs and motors. Temperature rise due to eddy currents was recorded using an IR thermometer aimed at the levitation plate. Voltage and current were monitored through the power supply display to estimate power consumption during trials.

Levitation starts at 50% duty cycle, with the air gap peaking around 80%. Temperatures on the aluminum plate reach 80°C with no load, and ~90°C with load, with an air gap of 14–16 mm. Current demand is highest at low duty cycles.
