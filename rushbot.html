RushBot Autonomous Rover

Field-ready wiring • Motor control • Maintainable electronics

As Electronics Lead for the RushBot project, I was responsible for the full design and implementation of the rover’s electrical system. My contributions centered on developing all wiring, and integrating the hardware needed to power 13 independent motors, a LiDAR sensor, and a Raspberry Pi–Arduino control hierarchy. I used Altium Designer extensively to create nearly all schematic footprints, wiring diagrams, and PCB layouts that tied together high- and low-voltage systems.

I also co-led the assembly of our custom 4s8p lithium-ion battery pack with an integrated battery management system, ensuring safe operation under the rover’s demanding power requirements.

RushBot ultimately won first place at the Theta Tau Southwest Regional Engineering Competition, serving as a showcase of collaboration across mechanical, software, and electrical subteams. This project bettered my skills in cross-disciplinary engineering leadership and embedded systems.
